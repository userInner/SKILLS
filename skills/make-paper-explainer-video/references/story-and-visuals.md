# Story, visuals, and voice

## The eight-beat script

1. “People say X. The paper did not actually measure X.”
2. “The researchers recruited N people and asked them to do Y.”
3. “They compared A, B, and C.”
4. “The clearest result was numerator/denominator.”
5. “The original figure also showed Z, but Z means only…”
6. “The surprising secondary result was…”
7. “Three limitations materially narrow the claim…”
8. “The useful conclusion is not prohibition; it is this workflow…”

Do not force all eight beats if the paper lacks a secondary result or practical action.

## Visual grammar

- Use warm gray desk or neutral page backgrounds.
- Preserve the paper's typography and figure colors.
- Use red for correction, warning, and the focal comparison.
- Use yellow only for the conclusion or one key term.
- Show one evidence object per shot.
- Zoom far enough that the relevant chart or paragraph is legible.
- Put explanatory captions below the paper, not on top of axes or legends.
- Use 2-4 second phrases rather than full narration transcripts.

Avoid glowing cards, animated neural networks, AI faces, fake terminals, generic stock, and decorative particles.

## Voice direction

Target “a thoughtful person who has read the paper,” not “documentary trailer” or “news anchor.”

- Low-to-mid pitch often fits skeptical analysis, but naturalness matters more than gender.
- Keep sentence-final pitch varied.
- Pause before denominators, corrections, and limitations.
- Do not stress every noun.
- Keep background music at least 15-20 dB below narration.

Generate a 10-second test when changing timbre. Compare intelligibility, sibilance, prosody, and listener fatigue before synthesizing the full script.

## Retention without distortion

Use conflict between public interpretation and actual measurement. Do not manufacture conflict between authors and data. The correction itself is the hook.

Strong: “This paper is quoted as proof that AI makes people stupid. It never measured intelligence.”

Weak: “A terrifying MIT study proves AI is destroying your brain.”
